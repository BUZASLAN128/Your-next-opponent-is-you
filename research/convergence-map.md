# Research Convergence Map

> Status: living, technology-neutral product model
> Last updated: 2026-07-16
> Authority: organizes confirmed requirements, research findings, candidates,
> and evidence gates; it does not select infrastructure

## Purpose

This file is the project's directional map. Each incoming report is preserved
and audited, then its useful parts are placed here in dependency order. The map
exists to move the research gradually toward a coherent system without turning
an attractive paper, named framework, mathematical expression, or agent
recommendation into an unearned design decision.

The target remains a private persona with communication-imitation and judgment
components. The first measurable slice asks what the user would probably
accept in a specific context, why, with what evidence, and under what
uncertainty. Persona resemblance, advice, capability, and authority remain
separate concepts.

## Evidence Maturity Ladder

A proposal may move forward only through visible maturity states:

1. **Incoming claim:** preserved but unverified.
2. **Source-audited finding:** traced to primary evidence with transfer limits.
3. **Technology-neutral requirement or candidate:** relevant to this project,
   but not yet shown on the user's corpus.
4. **Corpus-tested hypothesis:** survives annotation, leakage, baseline, and
   temporal evaluation.
5. **User-confirmed product decision:** explicitly approved with acceptance
   criteria.
6. **Implementation-selected design:** alternatives compared after
   requirements are measured.
7. **Runtime-verified behavior:** demonstrated at the evidence tier required by
   its risk.

Skipping a state must be recorded as an unresolved risk, not hidden behind a
confidence score.

## Functional Dependency Order

~~~text
Private ownership and corpus governance
    -> source receipts and raw evidence events
    -> human-auditable interpretation candidates
    -> scoped and reversible consolidation
    -> durable subconscious personality core
    -> bounded conscious workspace and mission state
    -> declared reasoning mode
    -> separately authorized external effects
    -> observed outcomes and corrections
    -> new evidence, evaluation, and possible revision
~~~

The system should not begin with a database, model, graph engine, container, or
agent protocol. Those are possible implementations of requirements that still
have to be measured.

## Layer 0 — Private Ownership and Corpus Governance

### Confirmed

- Public code and real personal identity must remain separate.
- Raw history and derived identity are sensitive.
- Local-first operation is required.
- Deletion, revocation, provenance, and inspectability cannot be optional.

### Required before retaining or interpreting content

- source-by-source ownership and consent;
- a metadata-only inventory;
- third-party and organizational-data boundaries;
- retention and deletion propagation;
- classification of which derived artifacts remain identity-bearing.

D-046 closes these questions only for one transient parser check: no content or
derived event survives process exit, no interpretation occurs, and structural
user turns remain unattributed. Durable evidence windows still require the
full governance contract above.

### Not decided

Containerization, encryption mechanisms, key storage, synchronization, cloud
participation, and deployment topology.

## Layer 1 — Source Evidence Plane

The first durable representation should describe what was observed without
claiming what it means.

Candidate source-event families include:

- conversation message or edit;
- quoted or forwarded content;
- assistant or tool output;
- file, document, or web-source receipt;
- user correction, acceptance, rejection, or question;
- intended action, actual action, and observed outcome;
- branch, parent, timestamp, role, and source identifiers.

Structural metadata is not epistemic truth. A message with `role=user` may
still be a quotation, joke, hypothetical, pasted model output, temporary
instruction, or third-party statement. An inactive conversation branch is not
automatically a rejected belief. Evidence classification requires content,
lineage, and context and must preserve uncertainty.

## Layer 2 — Interpretation and Identity Candidates

Interpretations must remain separate records linked back to source events.

### Candidate personal dimensions

- autobiographical episode;
- world belief or factual assumption;
- value or value conflict;
- preference and exception;
- norm, prohibition, or evidence standard;
- goal, mission, or abandoned goal;
- relationship model;
- skill or procedure;
- metacognitive habit;
- communication style.

These categories are useful annotation candidates, not a fixed stability
hierarchy. Stability, scope, and update behavior belong to each individual
claim. A belief may be temporary; a style pattern may be stable; a value may
have role-specific exceptions.

The fourth incoming report supports **Candidate Ontology v0.1** as four linked
analytical views rather than four isolated stores:

- probabilistic behavioral patterns or traits;
- value priorities and conflicts;
- autobiographical narrative and temporal continuity;
- personal metacognitive tendencies concerning uncertainty, evidence,
  questions, and correction.

Beliefs, preferences, missions or goals, relationships, and skills remain
separate scoped and versioned objects connected to these views. No view is
assumed to be the primary core, and no category determines its own stability.
The ontology must survive an annotation-agreement trial and a temporal
judgment benchmark before promotion.

### Declared-only persona preview checkpoint

The first implemented Layer 2 projection accepts only explicitly adopted,
source-linked declarations. It preserves exact statements, subject, scope,
decision label, source receipt, speaker, claim holder, authority, and data
class. It organizes traits, values, narratives, and personal metacognition into
the four candidate views while keeping beliefs, preferences, goals,
relationships, and skills as scoped objects.

The preview is deliberately incomplete: empty views remain visible,
confidence is `low_unvalidated`, protected-control semantic screening and
temporal stability remain unknown, and scope generalization is blocked. It
does not summarize, infer, persist, call a provider, grant authority, or
promote a core record. This is declaration apparatus, not evidence that the
four-view ontology is correct or that the represented user has been modeled.

### Atomic interaction-review checkpoint

The next Layer 1-to-2 boundary is now implemented as a pure, non-persisting
review projection. A provisional, unsealed interaction receipt preserves exact
available prompt and response evidence, response hash, source spans, speaker,
claim holder, source authority, source-only adoption state, subject, scope,
time precision, context, and data class. One receipt may support multiple
atomic proposals rather than forcing one sentence into one identity category.

Every proposal keeps speech act, modality, claim type, target layer, literal
normalization, inference, candidate consequence, three confidence dimensions,
and explicit null reasons separate. Project constitution, protected controls,
architecture candidates, scoped policy, mission state, episodic memory,
experiment backlog, research vision, and persona candidates are distinct
review targets. Source authorship is high-confidence evidence about who wrote
the span; it is not confirmation that the classification is correct or current.

The output remains `awaiting_user_confirmation`, `not_persisted`,
`authority=none`, and `automatic_core_promotion=false`; every claim is
`proposed`, confirmation-required, and ineligible for the core. The current
five-statement in-memory check produced 20 atomic proposals and only one
low-confidence persona candidate. This is manual review apparatus, not a
production extractor, annotation result, sealed receipt, or identity finding.

### Deterministic correction and decision-lifecycle checkpoint

V1.2 now supplies the next non-persisting transition. A canonical correction
receipt binds explicit per-atom user decisions to the immutable review digest
and optional predecessor digest. It supports partial review, eight bounded
actions, and explicit supersession. Both construction and replay revalidate
the action against the original source atom; a valid-looking recomputed hash
does not authorize an invalid split, scope expansion, time range, or core
request.

Replay preserves input order, fails closed on chain and boundary violations,
and projects current plus historical state deterministically. The decision
brief then filters by person, project, role, audience, risk, and time before
partitioning protected control, project, mission, episodic, persona, and
research layers. Applicable conflicts remain visible and force abstention.
Recency and repetition do not choose a winner. The current semantic-conflict
check handles only equal normalized literals with opposing modalities; this
limitation remains explicit.

No correction receipt exists for the historical private 20-atom set. Its
Git-safe form exposes only 20 pending slots, and the opaque local item **3B**
remains a pending persona
candidate with `core_eligible=false`. This checkpoint proves lifecycle
apparatus, not the truth of any atom, a durable memory, or a persona.

A later fresh private receipt has a distinct bounded proposal set. Its first
actions were applied before approval and retained after the user's later
ratification; the review remained partial at that point. The correction
receipt replayed deterministically
and does not inherit the historical slots. No proposal becomes identity
evidence merely because the local model produced a schema-valid record, and
post-hoc ratification does not rewrite the initial execution order.

The user later reviewed the remaining bounded batch through a receipt linked
to its predecessor. The selected review reached a complete state, full-chain
replays agreed, the earlier receipt remained intact, and deletion closure
remained projection-only. Exact outcome and dependency counts remain private.
This closes a review workflow, not the semantic or scientific validity
question.

### Proposal-only local extraction checkpoint

The model gate has now opened only for a bounded extraction experiment. A
pinned Qwen3-8B Q4_K_M artifact runs through `llama-server` on loopback and can
propose candidates into the existing interaction-review contract. The model
does not own source spans, scope, identity truth, correction, supersession,
conflict resolution, persistence, promotion, or action authority.
Deterministic code re-resolves every exact source occurrence, rejects malformed
or duplicate output, inherits the typed source scope, and records the model
revision and artifact digest with the review.

One D0 synthetic smoke test produced two valid proposals. A later private
current-thread run used an explicit outside-Git root and produced a bounded
pending proposal set. Its first attempt failed closed on a non-exact span and wrote no
review; the corrected request now restricts model-selected text to exact
deterministic source segments. Manual inspection still found a materially
unsupported interpretation, so exact spans solve provenance, not semantics.
This is not an extraction benchmark or a model-selection result. The earlier
20 private atoms were not persisted; this later run is a fresh review rather
than a reconstruction claim.

### Separate protected controls

The represented person's identity, privacy boundary, authority model,
provenance requirements, deletion, revocation, audit, and rollback rules are
system controls. They must not be treated as ordinary learned personality
traits that the system can rewrite through behavioral inference.

A descriptive metacognitive tendency and a protected control may use similar
words but have different authority. Evidence that the user often asks for
proof may support an identity candidate. A prohibition on fabricated reading
claims or unauthorized irreversible actions is a system guardrail and does not
depend on whether that tendency is inferred from the user.

## Layer 3 — Consolidation and Core Promotion

The current direction is a fast evidence layer and a slower durable core.
Recurrence may schedule review or consolidation work, but repetition does not
prove truth, user authorship, independence, current validity, or global scope.

A candidate may influence the active core only when protected gates pass:

~~~text
EligibleForCore(candidate) =
    source_receipts_valid
    AND represented-speaker authority established
    AND scope and time applicability established
    AND dependent evidence is not counted as independent
    AND high-impact contradictions are resolved or exposed
    AND explicit support or relevant outcome evidence is sufficient
    AND temporal evaluation beats required baselines
    AND privacy, provenance, and authority tests pass
    AND checkpoint, audit, revocation, and rollback are ready
~~~

No numerical values have been assigned. Different claim types may require
different evidence. A personal preference does not need two external witnesses;
a factual claim is not made true merely by direct user authorship.

One explicit, attributable user instruction may become immediately active in
its stated scope without becoming an inferred global personality trait.
Conversely, repeated behavior or a favorable outcome may remain ambiguous.
Source authority, evidence independence, temporal stability, scope, recency,
contradiction, behavior, outcome, and attack suspicion must remain separate
dimensions rather than one universal E0-E3 truth score.

## Layer 4 — Durable Subconscious Core

The core is a versioned, source-linked identity substrate, not a transcript,
single summary, fixed MBTI profile, or immutable list of values. It must retain
historical selves, conflicts, exceptions, scopes, and reasons for revision.

The core should provide evidence and constraints. It should not speak, grant
authority, or execute actions directly.

## Layer 5 — Conscious Workspace, Mission, and Retrieval

The bounded workspace reconstructs the current state from:

- current context and active mission stack;
- relevant core evidence;
- unresolved conflicts and unknowns;
- declared operating mode;
- available reasoning capability;
- explicit action authority.

Retrieval is a behavior to evaluate, not yet a selected technology. Semantic
similarity, temporal relations, associative reconstruction, exact rules, and
event history may each help different queries. Their value must be compared
against simpler baselines on the same tasks.

### Data-free Manager checkpoint

The first workspace may now start with no persona history and no database. Its
generated memory is deliberately split from identity:

- a deterministic D0 `system_control` operating seed provides evidence
  honesty, bounded questioning, reversible progress, and no-false-action
  rules;
- persona evidence remains zero and persona memory remains explicitly empty;
- the current task remains D1 private context, while guidance is generic,
  personal fit is unknown, and exactly one learning question is exposed;
- the seed is ephemeral, performs no action, and cannot enter persona
  retrieval or automatic core promotion.

This is an implemented cold-start control path, not a persona. The next
research boundary is how an attributable user response becomes a proposed,
scoped, correctable candidate without laundering manager text into identity.

Retrieval now also requires a positive lexical match after scope and stale
filters. An unrelated same-scope record no longer becomes persona evidence by
fallback. This is a conservative V1 guard, not a final retrieval strategy; an
adversarial retrieval benchmark must still compare unrelated, wrong-scope,
stale, and conflicting records.

## Layer 6 — Reasoning Modes

- **Mirror:** predict the user's likely judgment using permitted personal
  evidence.
- **Advisor or Mission:** pursue a better outcome while preserving the user's
  values and naming disagreements.
- **Copilot:** draft, critique, and prepare while the user remains the actor.
- **Observer or Learner:** capture evidence and propose lessons without
  self-granting authority.
- **Delegate:** act only under an explicit, scoped, expiring, revocable lease.

The mode controls the meaning of the output. It does not automatically widen
what data may be read. The report-level proposals that give Advisor all history
or Learner the entire environment violate data minimization unless a concrete
task and authority require that access.

## Layer 7 — Authority and External Effects

Persona confidence never grants permission. Read, analyze, draft, write, send,
execute, approve, publish, and delete are distinct action classes.

Execution history and authorization history should remain separately
queryable. Cryptographic integrity may help prove issuer and mutation history;
it cannot prove semantic truth, correct intent, legitimate root authority, or a
successful real-world outcome.

Some effects can be staged or rolled back. Others, such as a delivered message
or disclosed secret, are irreversible and need prevention, confirmation, and
possibly compensating action rather than a false universal rollback promise.

## Layer 8 — Evaluation and Learning

Persona fidelity, capability uplift, retrieval quality, continuity, privacy,
and authority compliance need separate measures.

The latest report sharpens persona evaluation into four minimum axes:

- judgment fidelity;
- temporal preference and belief updating;
- epistemic calibration and abstention;
- useful but explicitly declared divergence in Advisor mode.

Style imitation and authorship deception are not acceptance targets.

The first discriminating benchmark remains Mirror-only temporal holdout:

1. learn only from earlier evidence;
2. hide the later user decision and outcome;
3. predict decision, rationale, evidence demand, scope, and abstention;
4. compare against no-history, recent-context, static-summary,
   retrieval-only, and simple-rule baselines;
5. audit errors with the real user.

Automatic core promotion, external reasoning, and action authority should be
tested later in offline replay and synthetic shadow environments. Arbitrary
`80/20`, personality, accuracy, consistency, or leakage thresholds are not
accepted.

The implemented benchmark now creates a target-free predictor input that
omits hidden decisions and hidden rationale fields. Synthetic support fixtures
no longer repeat each case's hidden label in every input channel. Current
metrics cover classification, coverage, abstention, selective accuracy,
decision loss, and fatal-gate counts. Calibration, rationale overlap, and
evidence-demand fidelity are still missing contracts, and fixed synthetic
confidence cannot establish calibration.

## V1.7 Formal-Safety Convergence Checkpoint — 2026-07-17

The formal track now separates four answer bases and replaces scope ordering
with concrete query-environment membership. This removes two unsafe shortcuts:
unknown risk does not inherit a high-risk rule, and an uncalibrated model score
cannot become a personal prediction. Same-key conflict is three-valued, so an
unknown relation abstains instead of being silently treated as compatible.

Learning and privacy now have implementation-independent concurrency and
information-flow contracts. Review appends bind event ID, stream, expected
revision, payload hash, and causation; paired writers at one head have one
winner. Privacy compares the complete external observer trace across private
states. Erasure additionally requires producer-registry parity, post-delete
noninfluence, and a tombstone fence. D2-D3 identity data remains prohibited
from model parameters without separate unlearning evidence.

The evaluation center is now risk at matched coverage with paired
source-cluster bootstrap intervals. AURC is diagnostic and AUGRC secondary.
Chronological future, unseen project, low evidence, conflict/provenance, and
model/extractor version are separate shift strata, with worst-stratum risk
reported. This strengthens the falsification protocol but creates no persona
evidence: numeric thresholds and sample minima are not frozen.

The implementation/test handoff is deliberately non-authoritative. Candidate
interfaces and red tests are specified, but no source, test, schema, corpus,
model, provider, migration, or dependency was changed. The next implementation
gate is to make those tests fail against the current code, map each contract to
one owner, and seek separate approval before changing runtime behavior.

## V1.7 Adversarial Formal Review Checkpoint — 2026-07-17

A user-supplied formula-and-flow audit challenged ungrounded thresholds and
ambiguous mathematical notation. Independent review accepted its kappa,
entropy, authority-notation, JPAF, P3, and happy-path diagram cautions only
within their evidence limits.

The review rejected the report's numeric replacement for the explicitly
non-numeric `resolve` notation, its reverse-implication authority formula, and
its proposed execution, cryptographic delegation, budget-lease, rollback, and
provenance-snapshot flows. These would confuse research inputs with authority,
expand V1 action capability, or retain content across erasure. W3C PROV remains
vocabulary and validation support, not a control mechanism.

The adversarial pass also found gaps the report missed. Personal inference now
uses a deterministic dependency closure, so omitting a conflicting decision
key fails closed. Calibrated persona emission is distinct from ranking-only
