# Persona Evolution Checkpoint — 2026-07-20

## Outcome

The full-persona package now carries a separate, deterministic temporal
profile. It organizes two kinds of evidence:

1. repeated behavior-pattern candidates; and
2. time-ordered contextual transition candidates.

This profile is not a thirteenth persona layer. The twelve canonical layers
continue to describe what evidence is available; the evolution profile
describes how selected direct-evidence signals repeat or change through time.

## Evidence Contract

Only already-admitted direct represented-user atoms are eligible. Every output
preserves an atom identifier, evidence receipt, and observation time. Imported
project instructions, assistant text, third-party text, model output, and
unsupported identity inference are excluded.

A repeated pattern carries:

- its rule key and bounded guidance;
- total represented observation count and distinct atom count;
- first and last observation time;
- a non-probabilistic repetition band; and
- bounded source receipt references.

A transition carries:

- the changed dimension;
- previous and next contextual states;
- the evidence-bound transition time; and
- separate before and after receipts.

## Safety State

Every real candidate is fixed to:

- `scope_status=not_established`;
- proposal-context-only use;
- no semantic adoption;
- `adopted=false`;
- `core_eligible=false`; and
- `authority=none`.

The repetition band is a ranking description, not a confidence score or
calibrated probability. A contextual transition does not prove personality
change: it may reflect a different project, risk level, role, audience, or
temporary operating condition.

## Verification

Synthetic chronology demonstrates that a plan-first statement followed by a
plans-off execution statement produces a bound planning-mode transition. Tests
also prove deterministic hashing, direct-evidence isolation, absence of a
probability field, and tamper rejection.

The existing completed private corpus generated a non-empty bounded profile
and a new brain atlas without loading a model. Private content, paths,
identifiers, hashes, dates, and behavioral aggregates are not recorded here.

## Scientific Meaning

This checkpoint proves temporal representation, not persona quality. It makes
the next human gate measurable: the represented user can separately review
whether each candidate is current, historical, scoped, rejected, or genuinely
adoptable.

After that receipt is frozen, similarity should be evaluated on identical
sealed scenarios using at least three conditions:

1. generic advisor without persona state;
2. static current-persona context; and
3. temporal-persona context with reviewed transitions.

Support, coverage, abstention, tie handling, and acceptance thresholds must be
frozen before user ratings are revealed. Automated judges may assist diagnosis
but cannot replace represented-user evaluation.

## Remaining Gaps

- No evolution candidate has represented-user semantic adoption.
- Candidate scope and currentness are not established.
- No calibrated mapping turns repetition into correctness probability.
- No sealed comparison shows temporal context improves similarity.
- No candidate grants action authority or autonomous self-modification.
