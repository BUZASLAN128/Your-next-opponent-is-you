## Strongest Reusable Findings

### Observation and interpretation must be separate

The report reinforces that conversation structure, speaker role, message
content, and later interpretation cannot occupy one undifferentiated record.
This project needs at least:

1. source evidence;
2. an extracted claim with uncertainty;
3. a decision or correction event;
4. a candidate identity implication;
5. an independently recorded action and outcome;
6. a promotion or supersession event.

### Stability must be learned per claim

Values are not automatically global, beliefs are not automatically durable,
relationships are not automatically temporary, and style is not necessarily
the least stable feature. Each claim needs its own scope, time, exceptions,
support, and contradiction history.

### Fast and slow learning remain useful

A fast evidence plane plus slower core is a strong functional hypothesis.
Recurrence can reduce when expensive consolidation runs, but eligibility for
the core remains a separate epistemic and safety decision.

### Authority is not persona

The report usefully keeps external actions visible, but then sometimes lets
persona mode imply access or action. The corrected rule is:

> Persona predicts or advises. A separate authority lease permits an effect.

### Functional cognition is the correct public language

Mission recovery, uncertainty awareness, autobiographical reconstruction,
source-grounded memory, self-limits, and internal critique can be tested.
Qualia, subjective experience, or sentience cannot be inferred from those
behaviors.

## Quarantined Claims

The following claims may not influence product behavior without new evidence:

- fixed JPAF weights as experimentally optimal identity constants;
- a universal stable-to-temporary ranking for the nine identity categories;
- active user messages as direct user thought;
- inactive branches as rejected ideas;
- Markdown markers as reliable quotation detection;
- a fixed two-source promotion rule;
- blind decay of preferences or authority through an Ebbinghaus curve;
- parent-child conversation rollback as deletion or revocation propagation;
- temporal graphs as perfect causality;
- event sourcing as deterministic rollback of external effects;
- symbolic rules as zero-hallucination systems;
- all-history access for Advisor or all-environment access for Learner;
- DPRF self-revision as permission to rewrite the active core;
- ACF or path efficiency as causal error attribution;
- Docker, cgroups, regex, NER, or encryption names as a completed threat model;
- redaction as anonymity;
- P3 figures as universal privacy and quality guarantees;
- a global novelty claim based on combining the named frameworks;
- any claim of complete, conscious, or implementation-ready cognition.

1. private ownership and corpus governance;
2. tamper-evident, versioned source receipts and deletion-aware raw evidence;
3. separately stored interpretation and identity candidates;
4. bounded, source- and outcome-gated consolidation;

7. declared Mirror, Advisor, Copilot, Learner, or Delegate meaning;

## Guidance for the Research Owner

The research should continue, but each returned report should be treated as a
contributor to this map rather than as another complete architecture.

For the next reports:

- preserve the original output even when it is wrong;
- require direct source URLs and claim-level support whenever possible;
- keep conflicting conclusions;
- do not ask the research tool to choose a database, model, container, or
  protocol;
- bring the reports back before accepting their thresholds or integrations.

After the current report batch, the next concrete step should not be
implementation. It should be an operational glossary followed by a small,
branch-aware annotation trial and a Mirror-only temporal benchmark. Those
experiments will reveal whether the proposed identity fields are observable and
whether they beat retrieval-only baselines.
